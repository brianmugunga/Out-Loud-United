Economic Empowerment

Building Financial Independence & Economic Stability

Our Economic Empowerment program addresses the significant barriers that LGBTQ+ individuals face in accessing employment, entrepreneurship opportunities, and financial services. Through comprehensive skills development and support systems, we help community members achieve economic independence and stability.

Key Initiatives

  • Skills Development Training: Vocational training in high-demand sectors including technology, hospitality, and skilled trades
  • Entrepreneurship Support: Business development training, mentorship, and access to microfinance opportunities
  • Employment Assistance: Job placement services, resume building, and interview preparation
  • Financial Literacy: Education on budgeting, savings, credit, and financial planning
  • Workplace Advocacy: Training employers on inclusive practices and non-discrimination policies

Impact Goals

  • Train 200+ individuals in job-ready skills within three years
  • Support 50+ new businesses started by community members
  • Achieve 75% job placement rate for training graduates
  • Partner with 25+ inclusive employers

Rights & Advocacy

Advancing Legal Protections & Human Rights

Our Rights & Advocacy program works to create systemic change through policy reform, legal advocacy, and human rights documentation. We collaborate with legal experts, policymakers, and other civil society organizations to advance comprehensive protections for LGBTQ+ individuals in Rwanda.

Key Initiatives

  • Policy Research & Analysis: Researching gaps in current legislation and developing policy recommendations
  • Legislative Advocacy: Engaging with lawmakers and government officials to promote inclusive policies
  • Legal Support Services: Providing legal referrals and support for discrimination cases
  • Human Rights Documentation: Documenting violations and publishing reports on LGBTQ+ rights in Rwanda
  • Strategic Litigation: Supporting test cases that advance legal protections
  • Know Your Rights Education: Educating community members about their legal rights

Current Advocacy Priorities

  • Comprehensive anti-discrimination legislation covering employment, housing, and public accommodations
  • Hate crime legislation with enhanced penalties for bias-motivated crimes
  • Healthcare access protections ensuring non-discriminatory treatment
  • Legal recognition of gender identity and expression

Health & Wellbeing

Promoting Comprehensive Health & Mental Wellness

Our Health & Wellbeing program addresses the unique healthcare challenges faced by LGBTQ+ individuals, including discrimination in healthcare settings, lack of culturally competent providers, and mental health stigma. We work to ensure that all community members have access to quality, affirming healthcare services.

Key Initiatives

  • Healthcare Navigation: Helping community members access quality, affirming healthcare services
  • Mental Health Support: Peer counseling, support groups, and referrals to LGBTQ+-friendly therapists
  • Health Education: Workshops on sexual health, preventive care, and wellness
  • Provider Training: Training healthcare workers on LGBTQ+ cultural competency and inclusive care
  • Wellness Programs: Fitness classes, nutrition education, and stress management workshops
  • Crisis Intervention: Emergency support for individuals facing mental health crises

Health Priorities

  • Reducing healthcare discrimination and improving provider cultural competency
  • Expanding access to mental health services and reducing stigma
  • Promoting preventive care and health education
  • Building partnerships with healthcare institutions

Mental Health Support

We recognize that mental health is crucial for overall wellbeing. Our support includes:

  • Weekly peer support groups
  • Individual counseling referrals
  • Crisis intervention hotline
  • Stress management workshops
  • Community wellness activities

Community Engagement

We facilitate meaningful dialogue between LGBTQ+ individuals and the broader community through:

  • Public education campaigns
  • Community forums and dialogues
  • Cultural competency training
  • Media representation initiatives
  • Interfaith dialogue programs

Integration Support

Building Bridges of Understanding & Acceptance

Our Integration Support program works to build broader social acceptance and understanding of LGBTQ+ individuals in Rwandan society. Through education, dialogue, and community engagement, we create opportunities for meaningful interaction and reduce prejudice and discrimination.

Key Initiatives

  • Public Education Campaigns: Awareness campaigns to promote understanding and acceptance
  • Community Dialogue Programs: Facilitating conversations between LGBTQ+ individuals and community leaders
  • Media Engagement: Working with journalists and media outlets to improve LGBTQ+ representation
  • Cultural Competency Training: Training organizations, schools, and institutions on LGBTQ+ inclusion
  • Ally Development: Programs to educate and mobilize allies and advocates
  • Youth Education: School-based programs promoting acceptance and preventing bullying

Integration Goals

  • Increase positive attitudes toward LGBTQ+ individuals in communities
  • Reduce incidents of discrimination and harassment
  • Build a network of 500+ allies and advocates
  • Train 100+ organizations in LGBTQ+ cultural competency
