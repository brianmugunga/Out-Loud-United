About Out Loud & United

Our Story, Mission & Vision for Rwanda's LGBTQ+ Community

Our Story

Out Loud & United (OLU) was founded with a powerful vision: to create a Rwanda where every queer individual especially LGBTQ+ residents, refugees, and asylum seekers can live authentically, with dignity, and with full access to opportunities that enable them to thrive.

Born from the recognition that our community faces unique challenges in accessing basic rights, inclusive healthcare, and full social acceptance, OLU emerged as a beacon of hope and change, with a strategic focus on achieving economic empowerment.

Our organization represents the collective voice of a community that has long been marginalized. Through grassroots organizing, strategic human rights advocacy, and comprehensive community-centered programming, we are building resilience. Central to our mission is enabling economic independence through vital programs like vocational training, entrepreneurship, and livelihood support, alongside facilitating integration and access to essential services.

We are actively building bridges of understanding while simultaneously working to dismantle the systemic barriers that prevent our community members from reaching their full potential and securing an empowered future.

Our Approach

Community-Centered Programming

We believe the best solutions come straight from the community. Our programs are designed by and for queer individuals, including LGBTQ+ residents, asylum seekers, and refugees, so they directly address the real needs and priorities identified by our members. This approach guides all our work, especially in providing vocational training, entrepreneurship workshops, and livelihood support, ensuring our programs lead to genuine economic independence for everyone we serve.

Evidence-Based Advocacy

Our advocacy efforts are grounded in research, data, and evidence that clearly demonstrates the challenges faced by all queer individuals, especially LGBTQ+ residents, refugees, and asylum seekers. This evidence allows us to clearly show the positive impact of inclusive policies, particularly those that support economic empowerment and access to essential services.

Intersectional Perspective

We recognize that queer individuals may face multiple, overlapping forms of discrimination. Our work takes into account the diverse identities and experiences within our community, including factors like refugee status, gender, age, socioeconomic status (which ties into financial need), and ability, to ensure our support is truly holistic and effective.

Collaborative Partnerships

We work closely with other civil society organizations, government agencies, and partners including healthcare providers and those focused on economic opportunity to create systemic change. These partnerships help us maximize our collective impact and ensure our community members, including refugees and asylum seekers, get access to the essential services and support they need to thrive.

What We Do

Economic Empowerment

We create pathways to financial independence and stability for our community, including refugees and asylum seekers, through:

  • Skills Development: Providing practical vocational training and job-ready skills programs.
  • Business Support: Offering entrepreneurship support and microfinance opportunities to start or grow small businesses.
  • Livelihood Assistance: Providing employment assistance and workplace advocacy.
  • Financial Literacy: Teaching essential financial education to manage money effectively.

Rights & Advocacy

We work to protect and advance the fundamental human rights of queer individuals through:

  • Policy Research:Analyzing policies and collecting evidence to support inclusive practices.
  • Legislative Work: Engaging in advocacy and lobbying for legislative change.
  • Legal Support: Offering legal support and referrals when rights are violated.
  • Documentation: Reporting on human rights issues and abuses faced by the community.

Health & Wellbeing

We promote comprehensive physical and mental health for everyone, ensuring our support is inclusive of refugee and asylum-seeking communities:

  • Healthcare Access: Providing support to navigate and access safe healthcare services.
  • Mental Health: Offering counseling and support groups for mental wellness.
  • Health Education: Running health education and prevention programs.
  • Provider Training: Training healthcare professionals on LGBTQ+ cultural competency.

Integration Support

We build bridges of understanding and facilitate the smooth integration of LGBTQ+ residents, refugees, and asylum seekers into society through:

  • Community Education: Running awareness campaigns to increase understanding.
  • Dialogue: Facilitating discussions between diverse groups within Rwanda.
  • Media & Visibility: Working with media to ensure positive and accurate representation.
  • Cultural Training: Providing competency training for organizations that work with our community.
