About Out Loud & United

Our Story, Mission & Vision for Rwanda's LGBTQ+ Community

Our Story

Out Loud & United (OLU) was founded with a powerful vision: to create a Rwanda where every queer individual especially LGBTQ+ residents, refugees, and asylum seekers can live authentically, with dignity, and with full access to opportunities that enable them to thrive.

Born from the recognition that our community faces unique challenges in accessing basic rights, inclusive healthcare, and full social acceptance, OLU emerged as a beacon of hope and change, with a strategic focus on achieving economic empowerment.

Our organization represents the collective voice of a community that has long been marginalized. Through grassroots organizing, strategic human rights advocacy, and comprehensive community-centered programming, we are building resilience. Central to our mission is enabling economic independence through vital programs like vocational training, entrepreneurship, and livelihood support, alongside facilitating integration and access to essential services.

We are actively building bridges of understanding while simultaneously working to dismantle the systemic barriers that prevent our community members from reaching their full potential and securing an empowered future.

Our Approach

Community-Centered Programming

We believe that the most effective solutions come from within the community itself. Our programs are designed by and for LGBTQ+ individuals, ensuring that our work directly addresses the real needs and priorities identified by community members.

Evidence-Based Advocacy

Our advocacy efforts are grounded in research, data, and evidence that clearly demonstrates the challenges faced by Rwanda's LGBTQ+ community and the positive impact of inclusive policies and practices.

Intersectional Perspective

We recognize that LGBTQ+ individuals may face multiple, overlapping forms of discrimination. Our work takes into account the diverse identities and experiences within our community, including considerations of gender, age, socioeconomic status, and ability.

Collaborative Partnerships

We work closely with other civil society organizations, government agencies, healthcare providers, educational institutions, and international partners to create systemic change and maximize our collective impact.

What We Do

Economic Empowerment

We create pathways to financial independence through:

  • Skills development and vocational training programs
  • Entrepreneurship support and microfinance opportunities
  • Employment assistance and workplace advocacy
  • Financial literacy education

Rights & Advocacy

We work to protect and advance legal rights through:

  • Policy research and analysis
  • Legislative advocacy and lobbying
  • Legal support and referrals
  • Human rights documentation and reporting

Health & Wellbeing

We promote comprehensive health and wellness through:

  • Healthcare access and navigation support
  • Mental health counseling and support groups
  • Health education and prevention programs
  • Provider training on LGBTQ+ cultural competency

Integration Support

We build bridges of understanding through:

  • Community education and awareness campaigns
  • Dialogue facilitation between diverse groups
  • Media engagement and representation
  • Cultural competency training for organizations
